Believe in yourself or die

Do you ever feel like nothing will ever go your way? Do all your plans backfire? Is the man in the mirror laughing at you or worse feeling sorry for you? Well join the line buddy.

As of late I have run into a big blockade both personally and creatively. I feel as if I have lost my identity in finding myself. I got a job I love in a career I'm passionate about, yet I have become a husk of a person. I don’t go outside, I don’t call my friends or family and the only things that motivate me have been football. I can’t even watching a fucking movie these days. I became good at what I do, and got comfortable in my zone of being good. I don’t think I'm good at anything else, I got too scared to write my book or blog because I have so many talented friends who scorch me when it comes to the pen. I am too scared to form connections because of my off putting personality, mood shifts, and overall emotional demeanor. I feel as if I am a diva to most people, but behind these shades are pale tired eyes. Paralyzed by fear, I choose to let the time pass by.


Well, that’s what I would say, but well look at me now blogging about it in December. Something has had to have changed right? Well, not really I still feel the same way I said earlier, but I don’t want to. Maybe that is the first change? Anyone who knows me knows I'm stubborn and competitive to all hell. I'd rather lose an arm and be right than to accept a fault. I have let myself believe in being bad just to prove my fears right. In the end what has that brought me, other than justification for my lack of ambition? Just like Vegas I kept choosing the safe bets, but these bets are too safe and have given me negative payouts. I’m undefeated but yet I am in this hole. I proved everyone wrong, yet I stand alone. I can control the odds, so why should I bet against myself?


Psychologist James Maddux has stated that high self-efficacy is the key to accomplishing our goals, being successful and managing stress. To everyone that is pretty obvious I think, on paper it makes total sense, but in practice it is far more overwhelming to just hold onto hope. If you ask me, I believe hope comes from within and stays inside like a foundation for your soul. You surround your hope with feats, facts and love to help you build up, but the hope is what has existed before these things. If hope was there first then it is a fact that it can exist without the walls. Walls are meant to be rebuilt, all across the world we have buildings that are hundreds of years old, but they have been torn down and rebuilt, not from scratch but instead from the strong foundation of the past. No matter what happens to you, or what they take from you, they can’t take away your foundation. The key to finding hope is to first believe this fact.





To try and build without the foundation of hope is to build a tower doomed to fall. Don’t be fooled by those who tower over you, instead look inward and choose to connect to those with good souls. Oftentimes a collapse, especially a big one, can be detrimental to everyone around them. I have never been moved by the success of others, to me it simply doesn’t matter, I instead have always chosen to watch their actions and seek out who they truly are. My biggest blessing is my eyes, and the ability to read good people, this is something I am confident in and I have never shied away from giving flowers to those who deserve it. I encourage you to look inwards within your circle and seek out any positive or negative energy you may be receiving. Do the people in your life have dreams, passions, energy, and love? Then you are most likely in a good circle. Instead it is those who give you escapism, vices, fear and loathing you must be weary of. Some people can be a distraction and will tangle you in a web of problems and drama, those are the people you must keep at an arms length. The fastest way to lose hope is to lose your sanity by seeing the same cycles play out, try to put yourself in rooms with fresh and new things every once in a while.


Now back to the self, if you choose to accept the fact of your foundation then how can we grow it? Before we dive in I would like to preface this section with a quote from Homer… no not the greek guy, im talking about the simpsons. “Sometime when you least expect it, you realize that someone loved you, and that means that someone can love you again. That’l make you smile”. I like this quote a lot, in the episode Homer says that to Moe after he goes through a heartbreak. The character of Moe is a tragically lonely and cynical man, but in this episode he instead chooses to lean into the hope of love of the future and it makes him warm today. To me this is the most important aspect of hope, even when life is at its coldest or hardest we can still light a fire in our souls for a day that we don’t even know when we will see it, but we do know we can’t see it in the dark. The fire of hope isn’t just for warmth but instead it is for lighting our path, to walk in darkness is to eventually fall. Find the light within you and catch it.


I am working on being more hopeful, I want to build better relationships, write and find joy in things again. I know it won't be easy, I know I am going to mess up, I know I am going to get hurt somedays, but I know that in the end I will be looking back and smiling.

“I fucked up the béarnaise oh my god no no no why am I doing this!?: a true story”

To make a béarnaise you must take 2 egg yolks and whisk them until whipped in a bowl first then transfer to a sauce pan and whisk under heat until the color and texture changes to a light saucey yellow. Now with the desired texture slowly add room temperature melted butter roughly 125 grams slowly while still whisking with the other hand. If you whisk too slow or the butter is too hot or too cold the sauce will break and the eggs won't bind with the butter. Same result if you pour the butter too fast or too slow. The whole operation requires non stop movement, but most importantly control. Lastly once you have the butter mixed in successfully you will add your acid reduction. To make this take 50 ML champagne vinegar and 200 ML white wine and let it simmer with 2 diced shallots. Once the acid has simmered and reduced strain it and take the acid reduction and add it to your egg and butter mixture. Now lastly season to taste your tarragon, pepper and salt and boom you have a béarnaise sauce perfect for any protein you cook up! Now do you want to guess where I messed up? In the beginning of course! I forgot to properly whisk my yolks before adding heat and ended up with a yolk scramble cook. I also messed up my acid reduction by adding and burning my shallots before I added my acid. Overall I knew what to do but I had the order of everything all wrong. I had to remake it from the jump and in a restaurant environment that is all shrink inventory and would result in my ass getting yelled at. Stories are more than just the plot

I recently posted on my ig story about how much I hate talking about albums, and I actually got a lot of engagement on that tiny rant. It gave me the inspiration to actually sit down and think deeper on “why” I truly didn’t want to write on something I love so dearly, and after hours of dwelling it dawned on me many mainstream releases lack a story or any true cohesion to truly warrant discussion. I know this isn’t due to a lack of my own skill. If you follow me on tiktok you should have seen that I have recently got into the manga “Berserk”. I have posted 4 videos around 10 minutes each dissecting these volumes and giving praise and connecting with the characters and story of this amazing manga. Berserk was a story I got into when it was truly hard for me to feel anything. The only thing I had to keep me going was stories, hopeful ones, tragic ones, fantasy, reality, all genres I just needed something to grasp onto. I am thankful to be reading berserk and I am currently on volume 9 of the deluxe’s. In addition to Berserk I have watched some fantastic shows, movies and to bring it back to music I have listened to some truly phenomenal albums that I can write essays on just because of their stories and depth. It made me look back on my draft of my “albums of 2024” and take a deep and depressed sigh. I deeply despise the way mainstream artists and executives are approaching projects in all mediums. Just this week I read an article about Netflix executives telling writers to make characters announce their actions so that idle viewers can still catch onto the show. I think this is absolutely absurd and disgusting to the artform of television. I already was on thin ice with tv shows and their constant cliff hangers and cheap gimmicks to attain viewers but the streaming era had watered down every “must see” series to a marketing campaign for the streaming services that have the rights to them. Every show is made with extensive focus group testing, aesthetic appeal and virality in mind these days. Netflix even goes through the effort to constantly update tv show banners to encourage users to click and engage, but for me this creates the opposite effect. Instead of having unique and representative iconography for shows everything has become a homogenized conglomerate of dramatic faces you don’t even know staring back at you. I fucking hate this shit with my whole soul and finding something that doesn’t want to make me kill myself on netflix is harder than taking a piss in a minefield. I truly hate Netflix and what they are doing to the art of storytelling.

In this blog I will tackle literature, television, and music to argue why storytelling is important in all these mediums and why I think the mainstream is trying to steer us away from such things. This is a topic I have just recently found myself to be passionate about, but as the year comes to a close I have come to the conclusion that many of my frustrations with the world I inhabit stem from the dilution of art in the mainstream. It is true everything that comes out still has a plot/concept/theme, but do these things have anything that you can’t summarize in just one tweet? In a world of oversaturation and instant gratification many companies have come to realize that there is no value in investing into one big project that will just get drowned out after a week, they instead would rather try to attach onto the cultural psyche through small clips/songs/memes in order to stay relevant out of sheer vitality. It is nothing more than an artistic parasite that latches onto our culture until it has sucked every stream of revenue dry. We live in a world where Hawk Tuah can be a podcast, currency and clothing brand. There is nothing sacred and the top of the mountain is built on the carcasses of thousands of beaten horses.





The art game is low key deceiving in that regard. The longer I analyze and enjoy art the more I come to realize the “biggest” and the “greats” are nothing more than frauds and wolves who prey on smaller artists. Do I care? No not really, I still like Drake's music because it sounds good. I know he isn't the best but I don’t care. I don’t think the best artists should be the biggest, I think it is impossible to be great and appeal to such a wide market of people. You can be good this is true, you can have projects that sell like crazy and sound amazing even. I think Michael Jackson’s thriller is a perfect example of what I am talking about here, to me this is a perfect album, every song on there is a hit, it sounds good, and his vocal performance is spectacular, but I don’t know a single soul who has thriller as their favorite album. I love Michael Jackson, but if you ask me I think his best album is Dangerous or Invincible because he was telling a story. The burden of being the biggest artist in the world while also fighting against your own record label, being drugged and paranoid, the man had real demons he was fighting and the music post reflected a more dark side of Michael Jackson that I find to be raw and authentic. He was able to balance those dark themes with also his most human and genuine ballads. On “Invincible” he gives us the songs “butterflies” and “heaven can wait” which are some of his best romantic cuts in my opinion and tracks like “Cry” “Privacy" and “Invincible” are some of his most blunt lyrics to date. It is a shame it was his last project; his paranoia and fears turned out to be true. Sony blackballed Invincible and the doctors prescribed him medication while the media kept pursuing Michael Jackson which would ultimately lead to his early grave. MJ was a tortured soul if you ask me, and I truly think his run from Dangerous - Invincible was the most free he has ever been in terms of his artistic expression. This might be my favorite thing Fujimoto has ever made. This short one-shot story is a real-life love letter to films in the form of a manga. The manga opens up with a kid named Yuta who is filming his mother's death. It is a slow and emotional opening. Fujimoto uses this technique of repetition in manga panels in order to create this film reel effect that just adds to the feeling of it being a movie. Another thing that is cool is the use of motion blur and the camera quality in this section compared to the rest of the manga. It is all so very interesting and makes this manga feel more authentic. take a look at this



And boom we made it to the ultimate reveal, this was a film competition. the explosion was just part of the movie, and we get back to reality. but this does not stop the cinematics from existing as Yuta continues to film in the real world as well. with these next images please notice when the in-universe camera starts recording

The manga continues to do this through its run time and just gets wilder as we get deeper into the plot. I only wanted to speak on the first few pages because I do not wish to spoil anything. Please go read this, it is my favorite manga ever and I am so glad Fujimoto continued to use the repetition motif in Chainsaw Man Part 2, i love how cinematic his work is.

2) Music

Music can 100% be cinematic, as of late I have fallen deep in love with the idea of a concept album. a concept album is an album that focuses on one central idea and theme. these albums tend to follow a character as they go through a journey and we find the start and end of the album to be drastically different in terms of mindstates. "Mr.Morale and the Big Steppers" is a great example of a concept album with a character. the main theme of the album is generational trauma as well as external pressure. Diehard is him pleading that is not too late to fix himself, but when he is faced with the solution of therapy he sends a rebuttal of his not needing into therapy, as the next track "Father Time plays". This next arc of the album has rich spirit, celebrating what Kendrick has gone through and how he is thankful that he is who he is. This keeps him distracted from his problems. We are ultimately hit with a wall where we see that it is all a front, "we cry together" is an example of how his view of women and past trauma has changed his view of women and relationships. The final word on "We Cry Together" is to stop tap dancing around the conversation. Kendrick gives the listener the truth of not idolizing these rappers (and LeBron) and after every statement comes tap dancing. "Mother I Sober" is a track where he finally comes to realize that he does need help, the sober truth is a powerful one. Utopia: first impressions

Well the day has finally come, Travis Scott has finally dropped Utopia and with the wait came huge expectations from not only me but the rap fanbase as a whole, did he deliver? In my opinion, HE 100% DID and right now I would like to share a few moments and things that really stood out to me. This isn't a review or a deep dive, I just want to share my initial thoughts.

In the very beginning of the album being announced I was not excited at all, I was convinced Travis Scott grew off me. I always associated his music with my cringe ass high school years. Travis did something I didn't expect though, as we got closer to the album dropping he actually made me get hyped, this is one of the best album rollouts since the Kanye donda 1 era. So when that album dropped best believe I was hyped as hell. I got in my car with some homies and loitered in a backroad and let the music run its course.


As for the music itself, I was genuinely blown away by the production and variation. The album takes a more mellow and ambient approach to production for some songs and I feel these tracks play for a great contrast to the more hype rager songs I associate travis with. The first song sets the pace so fucking well, its exciting but dark, it evokes both excitement and curiosity. Modern Jam has a feature from the GOAT teezo touchdown and is produced by 1/2 of daft punk, i just think that's neat the song is easily a highlight for me. My Eyes is another track I really enjoyed, I thought frank ocean was on it for a second when I first spun it, The first half being this cold, slow and ambient like beat serves as a great breather for the beautiful transition into a more hyped up second half. Drake was on the album that song "meltdown" was really good too. Fein was a good song, a younger me would have loved it more, im kind of over carti, but pairing him up with sheck wes and carti doing a new cadence was a great move and made the song hype. That Beyoncé track sounds like a renaissance B-side, which I was all here for. I Know? is probably the catchiest song on my first listen and is one of my favs. I thought YB was on topia twins but it was just Rob49.... :( still a good song. Parasail a great breather track because the next track skitzo is just 6 minutes of hype, we had a good young thug feature and a nice beat switch. Lost forever is really really good one of the first utopia era songs to be shared around, but the album release version has a new beat during westside gunn's part which elevates the song to an upper echelon. looove is good love the dance beat but could do without the cudi verse, i hate when he does that cadence where he sounds like a bored cicada. K-Pop is a good song but it definitly feels out of place, bad bunny is still the goat though and ate it up. Telekenisis is INSANEEE future cant do any wrong and SZA at the end put this in my top 3 fosho. the last song was a good outro James Blake is a safe beat for a feature and the 21 verse was solid.


My overall thoughts on the album, I think its really fun and has potential to be my favorite Travis album, it embraces the darker sounds like rodeo but has the stacked hyped moments like astroworld. Sometimes though I feel travis gets outshined on some of the songs, even on a production standpoint while I liked the beats, I can definitely say they felt like they were made for the featured artists in mind more than travis. Even on some of the solo travis songs like gods country and thank god, it sounds more like a kanye song (well some of the songs are actually old kanye scraps so that makes sense). Travis is good though and does show his versatility on this album really well, I think it will take me more listens to point him out better.

Unusual Nostalgia

Nostalgia is the momentum that makes the pendulum of life swing from the past to the present. The fact that we are able mentally transfer ourselves into our own past, to experience some of the sweeter things in life is a feeling that has always been both endearing and tragic to me. In today's society, I feel that we tend to manufacture false nostalgia, which can lead to an unhealthy cycle of reminiscing on the past whilst being bitter about the future. This mindset has turned some of the most optimistic and ambitious people I know into shells of themselves with their only wish to go back in time.

Manufactured nostalgia is one thing that has started to irk me more as I’ve grown older. As an #official twenty-something-year-old, I can tell you that there is an increase in “2000’s babies” nostalgia on the internet and in my day-to-day conversations. From my Twitter timeline being full of Spongebob clips, to people trying to gas up those terrible Debby cakes we used to eat as kids. It has gotten sooooo bad these days that even things like the school light fixtures can make people nostalgic. In my opinion, though these things are nothing more than shallow memories that don’t really have much nostalgia because it was just things that were forced upon you. Everyone knows these things and grew up with them, so what makes them special to you? For something to be truly nostalgic, you need a strong emotional connection not just a shallow “Oh yea I remember this as a kid” answer.


Today I want to talk about unusual nostalgia, and why it's different than the oversaturated Twitter bait nostalgia we see in our day-to-day lives. First of all, what is unusual nostalgia? Well since it is a term I made up, I figure I am the most qualified to tell yall, it is a specific thing from your past that only you (or a small population) feel nostalgic for. Seems pretty simple right? But in my opinion, it is its exclusivity that makes it infinitely more meaningful.


As humans we are designed to find comfort in conformity, we want to feel we fit in with the people around us. This is why so many people find comfort and nostalgia in even the most mundane things of the past. The only true connection they have to it is that everyone else shares that connection. While I do value a shared experience in life, I find this form of nostalgia to ultimately be damaging because it can tend to alienate your present self from society. It is a mindset that allows you to adopt a “life was better back than” mindset. Picture this when we are in our 70s and retired are we going to talk about our office cubicles like how we talk about our high school desks? The answer should hopefully be no, because that would be really sad if we was nostalgic over that. I think school and childhood nostalgia is really easy to make for most people because, for a lot of people, it was a time when they felt the most social due to them not having to try as hard to make friends back then. This type of nostalgia is the junk food of memories it lacks effort and only fulfills you through the most artificial means possible.




For me, the type of nostalgia I value is far more unusual. The nostalgia doesn’t only take me back to my past but also helps build me up to be the man I am today. The personal experiences I hold dear that have changed me are what make up most of my nostalgia. I don’t hold high school in high regard, but I am forever grateful and nostalgic for the football team, and some of my teachers specifically my Spanish teacher Mr.Gale and my government teacher Mr.Busch. These people have given me memories and opportunities to grow and while I don’t look back at my time in high school as something I want to experience again, I find comfort in the fact that I can take a pleasant look at my past and come out with a brighter outlook on my future.


To get into even more obscure and weird nostalgia though, I will have to go even further back in my life to my childhood. This in my opinion is where I find my true nostalgia. There are three things that make me feel the most nostalgic, the PlayStation 2 startup sound, yellow bricks, and two albums, the first album being “the colour and the Shape” by the foo fighters and the other being a George Michael compilation album.


The PS2 was a staple in my family quite literally we would spend nights playing Madden or mortal kombat. I was just a little kid so I sucked at both games but I would always remember hearing that sharp start-up sound. It was a sound that was both frightening and enticing, as a child a lot of things seem more scary than what they actually are and nothing encapsulated my childhood fear like that sound. The irony with the fear is that it was all irrational, behind that seemingly traumatizing sound lay some of my favorite gaming memories ever. That sound and that experience shaped me in my future, I don’t really get scared rather I get excited more often. A lot of things that can scare you, can also be challenges and opportunities to do new things. That is one of the main reasons why I’m not a nervous or shy person when it comes to new experiences.



Yellow bricks are weird, but growing up both my parents would work so I would spend a lot of my early years at my grandma's house. She lived in this small little yellow brick house in a culdesac. Her house was like something I have never seen before it kinda reminded me of something you would build out of Legos. In a time when I didn’t know addresses or even how to read I found it importance of those yellow bricks to be paramount in my knowing where I was at. The idea that she lived in a place that felt like a landmark to me made me feel safe, even when I was away from my mom and dad. I was a clingy baby (big-time mama’s boy) so me being away woulda scared me, but something about my grandma and her yellow brick home always felt comforting to me. My grandma was dope and we would spend our time together watching cartoons, laughing, and eating. She would spoil the fuck out of me, I can’t even lie, I knew she was real when she would watch Ed Edd n Eddy with me, because my parents used to think a lot of my favorite shows were gross and weird so sharing that with her felt really nice. Every time I see yellow bricks I think of my grandma and I feel comforted, no matter where I go I can always find a home within my heart.


Lastly are the two albums, now if you are an avid or even casual reader of my blogs, then you should know by now that I really love music, but alas that was not always the case. Growing up I was indifferent to music the only things I heard were whatever was on the radio, my mom's Arabic music, and my dad's music. Now both my parents have implanted their music taste in me over time but I will say, as of late I have started to feel nostalgic and more connected with my dad's music. My dad's music taste is based a lot on the ’80s and 90’s music, growing up I heard a lot of A-HA, Foo Fighters, George Michael, Phil Collins, Prince, Michael Jackson, and of course the various random one-hit wonder bands of that era as well that I just can’t seem to name right now. Now I like all the artists I mentioned above, but the two that make me feel nostalgic would have to be Foo Fighters and George Michael. Foo Fighters is responsible for making my favorite rock album of all time, this is due to my dad playing songs like “My Hero” and “Everlong” all the time back in the day. It was the first time I had ever seen my father dissect the meaning and lyrics of a song and try to tell me something about it. Seeing him become passionate about a song was something I was annoyed by as a child, but as I grew into my own being I find that passion to mean a lot, especially coming from my dad who isn’t as into music as I am. It really felt like a full circle moment when the feelings my dad had for the music became the feelings I have for the music, and it makes me nostalgic for the days he would play that album in his pickup truck. The second album is George Michael’s “Ladies and Gentleman… the best of George Michael”. This album was just a compilation of some of his biggest hits. We had this one on CD and as a kid, I had no idea half the songs on here were about sex, but I really loved the music. This was the album we would pop in the car and drive around on a sunny day with no destination. Hearing songs like “Don't Let the sun come down on Me” as you are cruising in the back seat as the sunsets on the most scenic road you have ever laid your eyes on. It was a moment that felt straight out of a movie for me. What makes George Michael’s music special to me is that it is dramatic enough to make a moment special without taking you out of the moment. He has songs that I would argue are objectively perfect or at least perfect for specific moments. This CD made me appreciate music the same way I appreciate wine today, it is something that only elevates life. Overall I think the nostalgia for my dad’s music taste ultimately lies in the fact I am getting older and I have my own taste now, but whenever I overlap with my father in songs it always feels special to me. We are two drastically different people but even we can find some common ground and I truly think that is fantastic.



Nostalgia is a great feeling, I view it like an elegant dish rather than a tube of fast food that is constantly being fed to us. The feeling of nostalgia has to come up naturally through our own brain recognition, not just through some internet post trying to force positive memories to keep you complacent in life. Nostalgia is a feeling that should make you thankful for the journey because it brought you to where you are now, not because you want to drive back. The truth of the matter is for most of us are still young and can achieve many more memories that can fuel our future nostalgia, so let’s keep driving

YOU ARE LISTENING TO MUSIC WRONG

Hello everyone, sorry for the lack of posts I have been superrrrrr busy hahahahha but I am back #yipeee. During my little hiatus, I have been feeling a heavy amount of numbness and a lack of inspiration. As of late it feels like all my passions have been on the decline, I have been drawing less, writing less, and making music less. My lack of production on the things I love only added to my lack of inspiration which in turn only made it harder for me to get back into them. But after a well-deserved spring break, I am feeling recharged and excited to do things again. One of the things that made me find a new stride in my life is music, now anyone who knows me personally knows I love me some music. But before spring break I just found myself not even enjoying some of my favorite artists like I used to. After the Rihanna halftime show, I thought for sure I would stream all her music but I barely even felt the itch to touch her discog. Even Aubrey (drake) Grahm was someone I felt sick of, and I know that sentence made a lot of you drake #haters happy, but I got some news for yall now! I love music again (including drake ahahhahaha). So what changed in my life to make me enjoy these things again? Well, I personally believe there are 3 factors that affect your feeling about a song outside of the song itself.

The first factor is the scenery. Music is meant to be the soundtrack to our own lives and if our movies take place in the same 3 sets then we are bound to feel that our score is out of place. I love future but his music simply doesn't hit the same when I am on my college campus, or when I am listening to Call me if you get lost in the cold ass streets of Richmond I am bound to feel a disconnect to the music. Just like a movie score, our songs should enhance the places we are at instead of taking us away from them. For me personally, a change of scenery was necessary since 2023 started I have rarely been outside, but as soon as I went outside I found that a change of scenery enhanced my music listening so much. I was bumping that new kali uchis album on the beach and it was so gorgeous I genuinely felt like I was in heaven. You don't have to travel too far though to change your scenery though. Sometimes all you need is a nighttime drive, or a walk through the park to find a new appreciation for not just the music but life itself. One of my favorite things to do is go to the art museum and play Aphex Twin, it just enhances my experience so much more. Or when I cook I like to play bossa nova. So to conclude I feel a change of scenery can enhance your life and music so much more.





The next factor is movement, whether you are driving, fucking, or dancing you are still moving. Music in its base factor is built on rhythms and beats (even taylor swift, unfortunately) and our society has become more and more sedentary as time and technology have progressed. After the catfish finally passed all that remained was the pink kisser in the tank that doubled as both a graveyard and a prison. 14 years spent in the same four corners and everyone they knew they saw die. Matter of fact the tank they reside in is too small, when I educated myself more on fish care I realized that the pink kissing gourami needs to live in a 75-gallon tank while the tank I own is a measly 10 gal. Fish living in undersized tanks literally implode due to their organs growing larger than their body allows... yet mine still stands. This isn't a flex or me being a sociopath, I got this fish when I was just a kid and PetSmart markets Fish more as fashion accessories rather than a serious responsibility. Outside nothing changes and you can never truly control it anyways, the only thing you can control is yourself and the decisions you make.
